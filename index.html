How it began

Our Love Story

August 14, 2021

"The Stranger on the Train"

📍 LRT-2, Cubao Station, Quezon City

It started with a missed stop. Juan was buried in a book — Noli Me Tangere of all things — when he felt a tap on his shoulder. Maria, standing beside him, quietly pointed out that he had ridden three stations past his stop. Embarrassed but grateful, he offered to treat her to coffee as thanks. She almost said no. She's glad she didn't. They talked for four hours straight in a small café along Katipunan, and by the time they parted ways, both of them already knew something had shifted.

February 14, 2022

"Finally Official"

📍 Rooftop Garden, The Ruins at Taguig

Six months of long calls, shared playlists, and Sunday morning walks later, Juan finally asked the question he had been rehearsing for weeks. He had planned an elaborate speech, but when the moment came, standing under string lights with the city sprawled below them, all he managed was: "I don't want to keep doing this without you." Maria laughed — then said yes before he could finish. They mark February 14th not as Valentine's Day, but as the night their story truly began.

July 3, 2023

"Lost — and Found — in Sagada"

📍 Echo Valley, Sagada, Mountain Province

For their first big trip together, they hiked to Echo Valley and promptly got turned around on the trail back. What should have been a two-hour walk became five hours of wrong turns, shared granola bars, and increasingly dramatic theories about surviving in the wilderness. By the time a local guide found them laughing at the edge of a rice terrace, they had made each other a quiet promise: that they would always find their way back, no matter how lost things got. It remains their favourite adventure.

March 21, 2025

"She Said Yes at Sunrise"

📍 Naidi Hills Lighthouse, Basco, Batanes

Juan had been carrying the ring for eleven days, waiting for the right moment. He had planned it for sunset — but on the morning of March 21st, Maria woke him at 5 AM to watch the sunrise from the old lighthouse on Naidi Hills. Standing there as the sky turned gold over the Pacific, with the cold Batanes wind and not a single other soul in sight, he realised there was no more perfect moment than this one. He dropped to one knee on the grass. She covered her mouth with both hands, then nodded so many times she lost count.

January 17, 2027

"Forever Begins Here"

📍 Fundación Pacita, Sabtang Island, Batanes

And so they return — to the islands where he asked, and she answered. To the place that first showed them just how wide and beautiful the world could be. This time, surrounded by every person they love, they will stand on the cliff's edge and make it permanent. Not because love needs a ceremony, but because a love like theirs deserves to be witnessed.

Good to know

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I bring children?
This will be an adults-only celebration. Only children who are part of the entourage are invited to share in our special day. We appreciate your understanding.
Can I bring a plus one?
Due to limited seating, only guests named on the invitation are invited. We'd like to keep our wedding solemn and intimate with the most important people in our lives. No additional guests or plus ones, please.
Can I leave before the program ends?
We've lovingly planned every moment of this evening — especially the fireworks and same-day edit screening at 8:30 PM! We'd love for you to stay and celebrate with us until the very end.
What if I miss the RSVP deadline?
If we don't hear from you by the deadline, we won't be able to reserve you a seat. If you need to update your RSVP, please message us directly as soon as possible.
How do we get to Batanes?
The most common route is to fly into Basco Airport (Batanes) via Skyjet or Philippine Airlines from Manila. From Basco, you'll take a ferry to Sabtang Island (approx. 30 minutes). We recommend booking flights and accommodations early as Batanes has limited availability. A travel itinerary will be provided to confirmed guests.
